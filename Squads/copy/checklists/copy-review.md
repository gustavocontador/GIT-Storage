# Copy Review Checklist

**Checklist ID:** copy-review
**Version:** 1.0.0
**Purpose:** Universal copy review checklist combining principles from all squad agents

---

## 1. Foundation (Claude Hopkins)

- [ ] **Testable**: Can the effectiveness of this copy be measured?
- [ ] **Reason-Why**: Does every claim have a specific reason behind it?
- [ ] **Specific**: Are there concrete numbers, dates, percentages instead of vague claims?
- [ ] **No Cleverness**: Is the copy selling, not entertaining? (Unless entertainment IS the strategy)

## 2. Market Fit (Eugene Schwartz)

- [ ] **Awareness Level**: Is the headline matched to the prospect's awareness stage?
- [ ] **Sophistication Level**: Is the claim matched to the market's sophistication?
- [ ] **Mass Desire**: Does the copy channel an existing desire (not try to create one)?
- [ ] **Mechanism**: Is there a unique mechanism that explains WHY this works?

## 3. Persuasion Structure (Gary Halbert)

- [ ] **Starving Crowd**: Is there a hungry market for this?
- [ ] **A-Pile Test**: Would this piece get opened/clicked? Does it feel personal?
- [ ] **Story**: Is there a compelling story or narrative?
- [ ] **P.S. Sections**: Do the P.S. work as standalone summaries?

## 4. Template Compliance (Dan Kennedy)

- [ ] **PAS Applied**: Is there a clear Problem → Agitation → Solution?
- [ ] **Offer Present**: Is there a specific, clear offer?
- [ ] **Urgency**: Is there a reason to act NOW?
- [ ] **CTA Clear**: Does the reader know EXACTLY what to do next?
- [ ] **Benefits x7**: Are key benefits stated at least 7 times?

## 5. Flow & Psychology (Joe Sugarman)

- [ ] **Slippery Slide**: Does each element lead to reading the next?
- [ ] **First Sentence**: Is the first sentence short and compelling?
- [ ] **Seeds of Curiosity**: Are there hooks maintaining forward momentum?
- [ ] **Buying Environment**: Does the layout/format create a buying atmosphere?
- [ ] **Psychological Triggers**: Are at least 5 triggers present? (Authority, Proof, Scarcity, etc.)

## 6. Headlines (John Caples)

- [ ] **Self-Interest**: Does the headline promise a benefit?
- [ ] **News Value**: Does it announce something new?
- [ ] **Curiosity**: Does it make you want to read more?
- [ ] **Specific**: Does it use numbers or specific claims?

## 7. Readability (Bob Bly)

- [ ] **4S Formula**: Short sentences, Simple words, Specific details, Straightforward structure
- [ ] **Paragraph Length**: No paragraph longer than 5 lines
- [ ] **Subheads**: Subheads every 3-5 paragraphs
- [ ] **Scannable**: Can you understand the offer by reading only headlines and bullets?

## 8. Conversion (Joanna Wiebe)

- [ ] **VOC Language**: Does the copy use customer's actual words?
- [ ] **Rule of One**: ONE reader, ONE idea, ONE promise, ONE offer?
- [ ] **Seven Sweeps**: Clarity, Voice, So-What, Prove-It, Zero-Risk, Specificity, Empathy

## 9. Strategic (Todd Brown)

- [ ] **Big Idea**: Is there ONE central marketing concept?
- [ ] **Funnel Thesis**: Does the entire piece build one argument?
- [ ] **Offer Stack**: Is value built before price is revealed?
